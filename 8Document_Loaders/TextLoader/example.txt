Cricket is a popular sport played worldwide.
It originated in England and is now loved globally.
The game is played between two teams of eleven players each.
Matches are held on an oval-shaped field with a rectangular 22-yard pitch at the center.
The main objective is to score more runs than the opposing team.
Players use a bat and ball, with one team batting and the other bowling and fielding.
There are three main formats: Test, One Day International (ODI), and Twenty20 (T20).
Test matches can last up to five days, while T20 games are completed in a few hours.
The International Cricket Council (ICC) governs the sport globally.
Famous cricket tournaments include the ICC World Cup and the Indian Premier League (IPL).
Legendary players include Sachin Tendulkar, Sir Don Bradman, and Virat Kohli.
Cricket is known for its sportsmanship and traditions, such as the tea break in Tests.
The game features various bowling styles, including fast, medium, and spin bowling.
Fielding positions have unique names like slip, gully, and silly point.
Umpires oversee the match and ensure fair play.
Fans are passionate and stadiums often fill with thousands of spectators.
Cricket has inspired movies, books, and even its own vocabulary.
The sport continues to evolve with new rules and technology like DRS.
Cricket unites people across cultures and countries.
It remains a symbol of teamwork, strategy, and excitement.
